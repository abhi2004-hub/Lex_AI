COMMERCIAL LEASE AGREEMENT

This Commercial Lease Agreement ("Lease") is made and entered into on April 1, 2024, by and between:

PREMIER PROPERTIES LLC ("Landlord")
AND
FRESH CAFE LLC ("Tenant")

1. PREMISES
1.1. Landlord agrees to lease to Tenant the premises located at:
    123 Main Street, Suite 200
    Anytown, State 12345
    (the "Premises")
1.2. The Premises consist of approximately 2,500 square feet of retail space.
1.3. Tenant shall have use of common areas including parking lot and customer restrooms.

2. TERM
2.1. The lease term shall commence on May 1, 2024, and expire on April 30, 2029 (5-year term).
2.2. Tenant shall have the option to extend for one additional 5-year term with 90 days written notice.
2.3. Any extension shall be at the same rental rate as specified in Section 3.

3. RENT
3.1. Base rent shall be $4,500 per month, payable in advance on the first day of each month.
3.2. Rent shall be paid to Landlord at the address specified in Section 14.
3.3. Late payments shall incur a penalty of 5% of the monthly rent if not received within 10 days of due date.
3.4. Security deposit of $9,000 (two months' rent) shall be paid upon execution of this Lease.

4. USE OF PREMISES
4.1. Premises shall be used for operating a cafe and related food service business.
4.2. No other use shall be permitted without Landlord's prior written consent.
4.3. Tenant shall comply with all applicable laws, ordinances, and regulations.

5. UTILITIES AND MAINTENANCE
5.1. Tenant shall be responsible for all utilities including electricity, gas, water, and telephone.
5.2. Landlord shall be responsible for structural repairs and building maintenance.
5.3. Tenant shall maintain the Premises in clean and good condition.

6. INSURANCE
6.1. Tenant shall maintain commercial general liability insurance with minimum coverage of $1,000,000.
6.2. Tenant shall provide proof of insurance to Landlord within 10 days of execution.
6.3. Landlord shall maintain property insurance covering the building structure.

7. ALTERATIONS
7.1. Tenant shall not make alterations without Landlord's prior written consent.
7.2. Any alterations made by Tenant shall become property of Landlord upon termination.
7.3. Tenant shall restore Premises to original condition upon lease expiration, normal wear excepted.

8. ASSIGNMENT AND SUBLETTING
8.1. Tenant shall not assign this Lease or sublet the Premises without Landlord's written consent.
8.2. Landlord shall not unreasonably withhold consent to assignment or subletting.
8.3. Tenant shall remain liable for all obligations under this Lease.

9. DEFAULT
9.1. Default occurs if Tenant fails to pay rent when due or breaches any material provision.
9.2. Upon default, Landlord may terminate the Lease and regain possession.
9.3. Tenant shall be liable for all costs of re-letting, including reasonable attorney fees.

10. INDEMNIFICATION
10.1. Tenant shall indemnify Landlord against claims arising from Tenant's use of Premises.
10.2. Landlord shall indemnify Tenant against claims arising from building defects.
10.3. Both parties shall maintain appropriate insurance coverage.

11. HAZARDOUS MATERIALS
11.1. Tenant shall not store or use hazardous materials on the Premises.
11.2. All materials stored must comply with federal, state, and local regulations.
11.3. Tenant shall be responsible for any environmental contamination caused by Tenant.

12. COMPLIANCE WITH LAWS
12.1. Tenant shall comply with all applicable building codes and health regulations.
12.2. Tenant shall obtain all necessary permits and licenses for business operations.
12.3. Tenant shall be responsible for compliance with ADA accessibility requirements.

13. ENTRY BY LANDLORD
13.1. Landlord may enter Premises at reasonable times with 24 hours notice.
13.2. Emergency entry may be made without notice in case of fire, flood, or other emergency.
13.3. Landlord shall show the Premises to prospective tenants during the last 90 days of the term.

14. NOTICES

15. BINDING EFFECT
15.1. This Lease shall bind the parties and their respective successors and assigns.
15.2. This Lease constitutes the entire agreement between the parties.
15.3. This Lease may not be modified except by written instrument signed by both parties.

16. GOVERNING LAW
16.1. This Lease shall be governed by the laws of the State where the Premises are located.
16.2. Any disputes shall be resolved in the appropriate court of the county where the Premises are located.
