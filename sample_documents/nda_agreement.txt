NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

This Non-Disclosure Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into on March 10, 2024, by and between:

INNOVATECH LABS ("Disclosing Party")
AND
MARKETING SOLUTIONS INC. ("Receiving Party")

1. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION
1.1. "Confidential Information" includes all technical information, business plans, financial data, and proprietary knowledge disclosed by Disclosing Party.
1.2. Confidential information includes, but is not limited to: source code, algorithms, customer lists, pricing strategies, and research data.
1.3. Information marked as "confidential" or "proprietary" shall be presumed confidential.
1.4. Oral disclosures shall be considered confidential if summarized in writing within 30 days.

2. OBLIGATIONS OF RECEIVING PARTY
2.1. Receiving Party shall maintain the confidentiality of all Confidential Information.
2.2. Receiving Party shall use Confidential Information solely for evaluating potential business relationships.
2.3. Receiving Party shall not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent.
2.4. Receiving Party shall implement reasonable security measures to protect Confidential Information.

3. EXCLUSIONS
3.1. This Agreement does not cover information that:
    a) Is publicly known at the time of disclosure;
    b) Becomes publicly known through no fault of Receiving Party;
    c) Is lawfully received from a third party without restriction;
    d) Is independently developed by Receiving Party without use of Confidential Information.

4. TERM AND TERMINATION
4.1. This Agreement shall remain in effect for a period of 3 years from the date of execution.
4.2. Either party may terminate this Agreement with 30 days written notice.
4.3. The obligation to protect Confidential Information shall survive termination for 5 years.

5. RETURN OF INFORMATION
5.1. Upon termination, Receiving Party shall promptly return all Confidential Information.
5.2. Receiving Party may retain one copy for legal archival purposes, subject to confidentiality obligations.
5.3. Receiving Party shall certify in writing that all materials have been returned or destroyed.

6. REMEDIES
6.1. Receiving Party acknowledges that breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm.
6.2. Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief for breaches.
6.3. In the event of litigation, the prevailing party shall be entitled to reasonable attorney fees and costs.

7. DISCLOSURE TO EMPLOYEES
7.1. Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to employees with a need-to-know.
7.2. Such employees must be bound by confidentiality obligations at least as restrictive as this Agreement.
7.3. Receiving Party shall be liable for any breaches by its employees.

8. NO LICENSE GRANTED
8.1. Nothing in this Agreement grants Receiving Party any license to patents, copyrights, or other intellectual property.
8.2. All intellectual property rights remain with Disclosing Party.
8.3. No rights are granted except as explicitly stated in this Agreement.

9. GOVERNING LAW AND JURISDICTION
9.1. This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of California.
9.2. Any disputes shall be resolved in the state or federal courts in San Francisco, California.
9.3. Both parties consent to personal jurisdiction in California courts.

10. MISCELLANEOUS
10.1. This Agreement constitutes the entire understanding between the parties.
10.2. No waiver of any provision shall be effective unless in writing.
10.3. If any provision is found invalid, the remainder shall remain in full force.
10.4. This Agreement may not be assigned without prior written consent.
